INCLUSION/EXCLUSION CRITERIA
-----------------------------

Study Population:

The study included pediatric inpatients aged 2-12 years admitted to [Hospital 
Name], Paldi, Ahmedabad, between February 2025 and January 2026.


Age Range Justification:

Infants and toddlers younger than 2 years were excluded to minimize 
confounding from age-specific respiratory infection patterns, particularly 
the high prevalence of viral bronchiolitis in very young children, which has 
distinct pathophysiology compared to pollution-related respiratory illness in 
older children. Additionally, healthcare-seeking behaviors and admission 
thresholds differ substantially for infants, potentially introducing 
selection bias.

Adolescents aged 13 years and above were excluded due to physiological changes 
associated with puberty that alter respiratory vulnerability and immune 
responses. Furthermore, healthcare-seeking behaviors diverge significantly in 
adolescence, with lower hospitalization rates for equivalent disease severity 
compared to younger children.

The 2-12 year age range represents a relatively homogeneous pre-pubertal 
pediatric population with comparable respiratory physiology and pollution 
susceptibility, while maintaining sufficient sample size for statistical 
modeling.


================================================================================
STATISTICAL ANALYSIS - AGE CONTROL
================================================================================

Confounding Control:

To account for age-related heterogeneity in disease patterns and healthcare 
utilization, patient age (in years) was included as a covariate in all 
statistical models. Additionally, patients were categorized into three 
age groups for sensitivity analyses:

  • Toddler (2-4 years)
  • Young Child (5-7 years)  
  • Older Child (8-12 years)

This stratification allowed examination of potential effect modification by 
developmental stage while maintaining adequate sample sizes within subgroups.


================================================================================
EXPECTED REVIEWER QUESTIONS & RESPONSES
================================================================================

Q1: "Why not limit to school-age children (5-12 years) for greater homogeneity?"

A: While a narrower age range would reduce biological heterogeneity, the 
   2-12 year range provides:
   
   (a) Adequate sample size for stable model estimation (N=17 with partial 
       data; projected N=~800-1000 with complete dataset)
   
   (b) Greater generalizability to the broader pediatric population seeking 
       care at the study hospital
   
   (c) Inclusion of toddlers (2-4 years), who represent a substantial 
       proportion of pediatric respiratory admissions and may be particularly 
       vulnerable to pollution exposure
   
   Age was explicitly modeled as a continuous predictor to account for 
   developmental differences within the study population.


Q2: "How do you address heterogeneity between 2-year-olds and 12-year-olds?"

A: Three approaches were employed:
   
   (a) Age included as a continuous covariate in all models
   
   (b) Age-stratified sensitivity analyses performed to assess effect 
       modification
   
   (c) Discussion section acknowledges potential for differential pollution 
       susceptibility by age and notes this as a study limitation


Q3: "Why exclude adolescents if sample size is a concern?"

A: The marginal gain in sample size from including adolescents (13-18 years) 
   is outweighed by:
   
   (a) Physiological discontinuity at puberty
   
   (b) Fundamentally different healthcare-seeking patterns in adolescence
   
   (c) Low admission rates for respiratory conditions in healthy adolescents 
       compared to younger children
   
   Including adolescents would introduce substantial heterogeneity with 
   minimal statistical benefit.

LIMITATIONS SECTION - AGE RANGE
================================================================================

Add to your Discussion/Limitations:

"The study encompassed a broad pediatric age range (2-12 years), which may 
introduce heterogeneity in respiratory vulnerability and healthcare-seeking 
behavior. While age was controlled for statistically, residual confounding by 
developmental stage remains possible. Future studies with larger sample sizes 
could benefit from age-stratified analyses to assess differential pollution 
susceptibility across pediatric subgroups."
